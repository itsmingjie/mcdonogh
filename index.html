McDonogh School's First Open Hackathon

Learn. Build. Innovate.

When

November 10 & 11

Where

McDonogh School

What is McDonogh Hacks?

McDonogh Hacks is a 24 hour coding event spanning over 2 days where students will converge at McDonogh School to create amazing projects and learn about Computer Science. At the event, we will be having beginner to advance workshops and teams can compete against each other to win prizes that are judged by industry professionals.

Noun

hack•a•thon

hakə-THän

an event, typically lasting several days, in which a large number of people meet to engage in collaborative computer programming.

FAQ's

See some of the most asked questions about our event

Who can participate?

Anyone and everyone is encouraged to apply!

How much does it cost?

Nothing! Everything is free of charge including food and t-shirts.

What should I bring?

A laptop, charger, and any other amenities you need. The event is overnight so you will need sleeping items.

Will there be prizes?

Yes! Stay tuned for the prize distribution and categories.

What can I create?

Anything as long as it is school appropriate. You have full liberty to make games, apps, or websites. We encourage you to create projects that have positive impact. If you are struggling to think of ideas, visit chicagohacks.org to see winning projects and to explore another great hackathon.

What type of workshops will be there?

We will have workshops ranging from building your first website to machine learning. All workshops will be on Saturday and Sunday.
